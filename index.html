Help & Guidelines

Jaundice Screening Guidance

Clinical reference for healthcare workers

How to Position the Device
  • Hold device perpendicular to skin, ≤5 cm away
  • Ensure uniform natural or clinical white light
  • Avoid shadows and glare on capture zone
  • For sclera: gently open eyelid under good lighting
  • Preferred sites: forehead, sternum, palm, sclera
Risk Interpretation
LOW Score 0–4: Routine monitoring. Reassess in 48h.
MOD Score 4–8: Rescreen in 24h, monitor bilirubin.
HIGH Score 8+: Immediate referral to hospital. Initiate phototherapy protocols.
When to Refer
  • High Risk score (≥8) on any single screening
  • Visible sclera yellowing regardless of score
  • Preterm (<35 weeks) with any risk indicator
  • Moderate risk in newborns <72 hours old
  • Failed rescreen after 24h monitoring
Safety Notes
  • This app is a screening aid, not a diagnostic tool
  • Clinical judgment must always supersede app result
  • Ensure device is calibrated monthly
  • Disinfect device housing between patients
  • Do not use artificial or filtered light sources
